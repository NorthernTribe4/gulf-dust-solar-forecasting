Month-blocked CV ablation: notes and interpretation
===================================================
Run: src/train_dust_aware_cv.py. Leave-one-month-out CV (12 folds), daytime only,
GHI 1 h ahead. Features, models and seeds are reused unchanged from features.py
and modeling.py; only the evaluation protocol changed from the step-2 single
split.

The design change did what it was meant to: real dust events are now in the
held-out data. Pooling the 12 folds gives 17,618 daytime test rows (each month
held out once), of which 1,933 (11.0%) exceed the annual high-dust threshold
(dust AOD at t+1h > 0.4413). The step-2 Oct-Dec test had essentially none of
those, so the dust feature is finally being tested against real dust storms
(Mar-Aug).

The result is the same: dust still does not help, and slightly hurts. Pooled
all-sky RMSE improvement (dust-aware minus baseline) is -1.44% for LightGBM and
-1.74% for XGBoost. On the true high-dust slice (>0.4413) it is -2.06% and -4.24%.
Both models still beat smart persistence (skill around 0.22 all-sky), but the dust
feature adds nothing on top of that and is net negative on dust events.

Per-month RMSE improvement (LightGBM / XGBoost):
  Jan -4.8 / -0.4   Feb +3.5 / +3.1   Mar +2.5 / -2.3   Apr +1.0 / -0.4
  May -19.3 / -26.9  (max dust 1.14, 661 high-dust rows; the dustiest month and
                      also the worst for dust)
  Jun +25.7 / +10.7  (moderate dust; the only clear gain)
  Jul -0.1 / +0.6   Aug -0.5 / +1.5
  Sep-Dec: small, mixed, mostly slightly negative (little dust).
There is no consistent dust-driven improvement. The dustiest month shows the
largest degradation, and the one clear gain (June) is not a particularly dusty
month.

The likely reason is redundancy, not a tuning problem. The dust-blind baseline
already uses the observed clear-sky index kt = GHI/GHI_cs at 15-minute lags
(kt_lag0 through kt_lag4, kt_roll4_mean). That index is a direct measurement of
total sky attenuation, clouds and dust together, as seen at issue time. CAMS dust
AOD is a coarser 3-hourly, forward-filled model estimate of just one part of that
attenuation, so it largely repeats what kt already carries and mostly adds noise
the trees overfit. That is worst during the extreme May episode. Dust feature
importance was low in step 2 as well (gain ranks #10 to #17 of 29), which fits.

So the step-2 null was not just an artifact of a dust-free test window. Under a
fair month-blocked CV that does contain real dust storms, time-t CAMS dust AOD
gives no 1 h GHI forecast skill and is mildly harmful, most of all on the biggest
dust event. For this feature set and horizon, that is a solid negative result.

Things not tested here, left for later:
  - Longer horizons (3-6 h), where observed-kt persistence weakens and a dust
    signal might carry more information.
  - A setting without observed kt (pure NWP or clear-sky inputs), where dust AOD
    would not be redundant with a measured attenuation index.
  - Finer-resolution or forecast dust products instead of 3-hourly reanalysis.
