MONTH-BLOCKED CV ABLATION - NOTES AND HONEST INTERPRETATION
===========================================================
Run: src/train_dust_aware_cv.py. Leave-one-month-out CV (12 folds), daytime only,
1 h-ahead GHI. Features/models/seeds reused verbatim (features.py, modeling.py);
ONLY the evaluation protocol changed vs step 2.

DESIGN FIX WORKED: real dust events are now in held-out data.
  Pooled daytime test rows: 17,618 (each month held out once).
  Pooled high-dust daytime rows (dust AOD(t+1h) > 0.4413): 1,933 (11.0%),
  vs ~0 in the step-2 Oct-Dec-only test. So the dust feature is now genuinely
  tested against real dust storms (Mar-Aug).

HEADLINE RESULT: dust STILL does not help; it slightly hurts.
  Pooled all-sky   RMSE improvement (dust-aware - baseline):
    LightGBM -1.44% | XGBoost -1.74%
  Pooled TRUE high-dust slice (>0.4413) RMSE improvement:
    LightGBM -2.06% | XGBoost -4.24%
  Models still beat smart persistence (skill ~0.22 all-sky) -- but the dust
  feature adds nothing on top, and is net negative on dust events.

PER-MONTH (LightGBM / XGBoost RMSE improvement %):
  Jan -4.8 /-0.4 | Feb +3.5/+3.1 | Mar +2.5/-2.3 | Apr +1.0/-0.4 |
  May -19.3/-26.9 (max dust 1.14, 661 high-dust rows)  <-- dustiest month, WORST
  Jun +25.7/+10.7 (moderate dust)  <-- the only clear gain
  Jul -0.1/+0.6 (dusty, 627 high-dust) | Aug -0.5/+1.5 |
  Sep..Dec: small mixed/negative (little dust).
  => No consistent dust-driven improvement. The single dustiest month (May)
  shows the LARGEST degradation; the one clear gain (June) is not the dustiest.

LIKELY MECHANISM (interpretation, not a tuning excuse):
  The dust-blind baseline already uses the observed clear-sky index
  kt = GHI/GHI_cs at 15-min lags (kt_lag0..4, kt_roll4_mean). kt is a direct
  MEASUREMENT of total sky attenuation -- clouds AND dust -- as actually
  observed at issue time. CAMS dust AOD is a coarser (3-hourly, forward-filled)
  MODEL estimate of just one attenuation component, and is largely redundant
  with kt. Adding it mostly injects noise the gradient-boosted trees overfit,
  which is worst during the extreme May dust episode. Dust feature importance
  was low in step 2 (gain ranks #10-#17 of 29), consistent with this.

CONCLUSION: The step-2 null was NOT merely an artifact of a dust-free test
window. Under a fair month-blocked CV that DOES contain real dust storms, adding
time-t CAMS dust AOD provides no short-horizon (1 h) GHI forecast skill and is
mildly harmful, most notably on the largest dust event. This is a robust
null/negative finding for this feature set and horizon.

NOT TESTED HERE (candidate next steps, deliberately not run):
  * Longer horizons (3-6 h) where observed-kt persistence decays and a dust
    forecast might carry more information.
  * A setting WITHOUT observed kt (pure NWP/clear-sky inputs), where dust AOD is
    not redundant with a measured attenuation index.
  * Finer-resolution or forecast dust products instead of 3-hourly reanalysis.
