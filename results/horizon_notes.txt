HORIZON SWEEP (1/3/6 h) - NOTES AND HONEST INTERPRETATION
=========================================================
Run: src/horizon_sweep.py. Same month-blocked leave-one-month-out CV, models,
seeds, features as step 3; ONLY the horizon changes (4/12/24 steps). features.py
and modeling.py reused verbatim; train_dust_aware_cv.py imported unmodified.

REPRODUCTION CHECK (1 h) vs step 3: EXACT PASS.
  LightGBM baseline all-sky RMSE 44.585, skill 0.229; XGBoost 44.502, skill 0.230;
  pooled daytime rows 17,618; high-dust rows 1,933. Nothing regressed.

BASELINE SKILL vs PERSISTENCE rises steeply with horizon (context):
  1 h: ~0.23 | 3 h: ~0.65 | 6 h: ~0.80.
  As expected: smart persistence decays fast, so the ML model's structural
  features (clear-sky GHI, solar geometry, diurnal) dominate more at long range.
  This is the precondition for the dust hypothesis (kt-persistence weakening).

RMSE IMPROVEMENT FROM DUST (%, + = dust helps):
  horizon   all-sky (LGBM / XGB)    high-dust slice (LGBM / XGB)
   1 h        -1.44 / -1.74            -2.06 / -4.24
   3 h        -0.08 / +1.25            -0.60 / +0.48
   6 h        +2.19 / +6.91            -5.45 / -1.93

HONEST READING - a SPLIT / PARTIAL result:
  (a) ALL-SKY: the dust benefit DOES grow with horizon. It goes from harmful at
      1 h, to ~neutral at 3 h, to positive at 6 h (XGBoost +6.9%, LightGBM +2.2%;
      6 h all-sky RMSE 84.2->78.4 XGB, 81.0->79.3 LGBM). This is consistent with
      the hypothesis: once observed-kt persistence has decayed, an explicit
      slowly-varying dust/atmospheric-state signal starts to help general
      prediction.
  (b) TRUE HIGH-DUST SLICE (>0.4413): dust does NOT help at ANY horizon. It is
      negative at 1 h, ~flat at 3 h, and negative again at 6 h (LGBM -5.4%,
      XGB -1.9%). The 6 h all-sky gain is therefore concentrated OUTSIDE genuine
      dust events, which is the OPPOSITE of the original dust-storm motivation.

INTERPRETATION: At 6 h, CAMS dust AOD behaves more like a useful general
atmospheric-state / seasonal covariate (improving average all-sky accuracy once
persistence is weak) than as a dust-EVENT predictor. On actual high-dust
timesteps the coarse 3-hourly reanalysis dust does not pin down the highly
variable dust-storm GHI and can mislead (the extreme May 2022 event again drives
much of the high-dust degradation). So "dust helps more at longer horizons" is
TRUE for all-sky accuracy but FALSE for the dust-event slice it was meant to help.

BOTTOM LINE: The hypothesis is only PARTIALLY supported. Explicit dust AOD adds
modest all-sky value at 6 h (clearest for XGBoost), but provides no benefit on
true dust events at any tested horizon. Not tuned per horizon; no reshuffling.

CAVEATS / possible next steps (not run): finer-resolution or FORECAST dust
products (vs 3-hourly reanalysis) may capture dust-event GHI better; horizons
beyond 6 h; a pure-NWP setting without observed kt.
