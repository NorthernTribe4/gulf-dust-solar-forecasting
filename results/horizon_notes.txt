Horizon sweep (1/3/6 h): notes and interpretation
=================================================
Run: src/horizon_sweep.py. Same month-blocked leave-one-month-out CV, models,
seeds and features as the CV step; only the horizon changes (4, 12 and 24 steps).
features.py and modeling.py are reused unchanged, and train_dust_aware_cv.py is
imported without modification.

The 1 h reproduction check against the CV step passes exactly: LightGBM baseline
all-sky RMSE 44.585, skill 0.229; XGBoost 44.502, skill 0.230; pooled daytime rows
17,618; high-dust rows 1,933. Nothing regressed.

Baseline skill against persistence climbs sharply with horizon, from about 0.23 at
1 h to 0.65 at 3 h to 0.80 at 6 h. Smart persistence decays quickly, so the
model's structural features (clear-sky GHI, solar geometry, diurnal terms) matter
more at longer range. That is exactly the setting in which an explicit dust
feature could start to pay off, since the observed clear-sky index is a weaker
proxy once persistence has faded.

RMSE improvement from dust (%, positive means dust helps):
  horizon   all-sky (LGBM / XGB)    high-dust slice (LGBM / XGB)
   1 h        -1.44 / -1.74            -2.06 / -4.24
   3 h        -0.08 / +1.25            -0.60 / +0.48
   6 h        +2.19 / +6.91            -5.45 / -1.93

The result is split. On the all-sky set the dust benefit does grow with horizon,
from harmful at 1 h, to about neutral at 3 h, to positive at 6 h (XGBoost +6.9%,
LightGBM +2.2%; at 6 h the all-sky RMSE goes 84.2 to 78.4 for XGBoost and 81.0 to
79.3 for LightGBM). That matches the idea that once observed-kt persistence has
decayed, a slowly varying dust or atmospheric-state signal starts to help general
prediction. But on the true high-dust slice (>0.4413) dust does not help at any
horizon: negative at 1 h, roughly flat at 3 h, negative again at 6 h (LightGBM
-5.4%, XGBoost -1.9%). So the 6 h all-sky gain sits outside the genuine dust
events, which is the opposite of what the dust-storm motivation predicted.

Reading it plainly: at 6 h, CAMS dust AOD acts more like a general
atmospheric-state or seasonal covariate that lifts average all-sky accuracy once
persistence is weak, rather than a dust-event predictor. On actual high-dust
timesteps the coarse 3-hourly reanalysis does not pin down the highly variable
dust-storm GHI and can mislead, and the extreme May 2022 event again drives much
of the high-dust degradation. The claim that dust helps more at longer horizons is
true for all-sky accuracy and false for the dust-event slice it was supposed to
help.

So the hypothesis holds only partway. Explicit dust AOD adds modest all-sky value
at 6 h, clearest for XGBoost, but gives no benefit on true dust events at any
tested horizon. Nothing was tuned per horizon and nothing was reshuffled.

Left for later: finer-resolution or forecast dust products instead of 3-hourly
reanalysis, which might capture dust-event GHI better; horizons beyond 6 h; and a
pure-NWP setting without an observed clear-sky index.
