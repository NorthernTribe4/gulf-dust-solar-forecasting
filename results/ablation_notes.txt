Dust-aware ablation: notes and interpretation
=============================================
Run: src/train_dust_aware.py. Target is GHI 1 h ahead, evaluated on the daytime
Oct-Dec 2022 hold-out. Baseline and dust-aware differ only by the appended dust
features, and the baseline reproduces src/train_baseline.py to the digit
(LightGBM all-sky RMSE 42.30, XGBoost 42.19).

Result: no reliable improvement from dust. RMSE change (dust-aware minus
baseline, positive means dust helps):
  LightGBM  all-sky  : -1.85 W/m^2  (-4.4%), dust slightly worse
  LightGBM  high-dust: -1.44 W/m^2  (-2.4%), dust slightly worse
  XGBoost   all-sky  : +0.69 W/m^2  (+1.6%), negligible
  XGBoost   high-dust: -0.07 W/m^2  (-0.1%), flat
Every effect is about 1-2 W/m^2 on an RMSE of 42-60, which is run-to-run noise.
The idea that the improvement should be larger on the high-dust slice does not
hold up. The dust features rank low by gain (LightGBM #10/#11/#14, XGBoost
#16/#17/#22 out of 29); clear-sky GHI, solar geometry and the recent clear-sky
index carry the model.

Why this happens is mostly about the split. Training on Jan-Sep and testing on
Oct-Dec puts the whole Gulf dust season in the training set and leaves a low-dust
winter to test on:
  Annual dust AOD : mean 0.199, p90 0.4413, max 1.143
  Train (Jan-Sep) : mean 0.240, p90 0.4775, max 1.143
  Test  (Oct-Dec) : mean 0.077, p90 0.1333, max 0.294   (barely any dust)
  Monthly mean    : Mar 0.26, Apr 0.29, May 0.41, Jul 0.40 against Oct 0.08,
                    Nov 0.09, Dec 0.06
  Of the 3504 annual high-dust (>0.4413) timesteps, 0 land in Oct-Dec.
So the high-dust test slice (dust > 0.1333, n=394) is just the dustiest tenth of
a clean winter, topping out at 0.294. There are no real dust storms in the
hold-out for the feature to help with, so there is nothing to measure.

The takeaway: on this Jan-Sep/Oct-Dec split, adding CAMS dust AOD does not improve
1 h-ahead GHI, because the test period has no significant dust events. That is a
limitation of the evaluation window, not evidence that dust does not matter for
GHI. A fair test needs a hold-out that actually contains dust storms, for example
a spring or summer test month or seasonal cross-validation. I left the split fixed
here on purpose so the baseline comparison stays controlled; changing it is the
next step.
